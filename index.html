Dorothy Vaughan

was the first African American Mathematician and Programmer who leaded the Analysis and
Computing Division at NASA. She was the first Black supervisor at the NACA and one of the
first few female supervisors. Vaughan became proficient with computer programming, teaching herself
FORTRAN and teaching it to her co-workers to prepare for the transition. She contributed to the
space program through her work on the Scout Launch Vehicle Program.

Timeline of Dorothy's life

  • 1910 - Born in Kansas City, Missouri.
  • 1925 - Graduated High School, receiving a full-tuition scholarship.
  • 1929 - Graduated at the age of 19 in Mathematics from Wilberforce University, a historically black college.
  • 1932 - Married Howard Vaughan and started he career as a Mathematics teacher.
  • 1943 - Vaughan started to work for NACA, she was assigned to the West Area Computers at the Langley Research Center in Virginia.
  • 1949 - Vaughan took over as the head of the West Area Computing Division.
  • 1958 - Vaughan continued after NASA, the successor agency to NACA was established in 1958.
  • 1961 - Vaughan moved into the area of computing after NASA introduced the first non-human computers to the center.
  • 1971 - She retired from NASA at the age of 61.
  • 2008 - Vaughan passed away in 2008 at the age of 98.
